# 1. Executive Summary

Gabay Sr. is a mobile-first application designed to address two interlinked problems affecting Filipino senior citizens: **social isolation** and **vulnerability to online scams**. Rather than treating these as separate features, the product is built around a single mechanism — a **Trusted Circle** of family members and volunteers who remain digitally present around the senior, providing both companionship and a protective layer against fraud.

This document serves as the single source of truth for the development team building Version 1 (MVP) of Gabay Sr. for the SparkFest 2026 Elimination Round and Final Pitch. It covers the problem context, requirements, system architecture, data model, API contracts, UI/UX flows, sprint plan, and testing strategy.

## 1.1 Problem Statement

> How might we keep Filipino senior citizens digitally connected to the people who care about them — so they feel less alone, and so someone is always watching their back against scams?

## 1.2 Target Users

| User Type | Role in System | Primary Need |
|---|---|---|
| Senior Citizen | Primary beneficiary, assisted or direct user | Companionship, protection, dignity |
| Family Member / OFW relative | Trusted Circle member, remote monitor | Peace of mind, real-time visibility |
| Barangay Volunteer / OSCA Staff | Trusted Circle member, local support | Tools to assist multiple seniors efficiently |

## 1.3 Core Value Proposition

- Reduces loneliness through daily check-ins and AI-generated companionship summaries shared with family
- Protects against scams through real-time alerts to a Trusted Circle when suspicious activity is detected
- Designed for **assisted use** — does not require the senior to be digitally literate
- Localized for the Filipino context: Taglish-aware scam detection, OSCA-linked reporting, barangay-level deployment readiness
